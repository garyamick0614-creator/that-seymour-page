Seymour Weekly Update
Seymour is heading into the end of April with a busy mix of community events, city business, and storm cleanup still on many people’s minds. The biggest recent story has been the severe weather that moved through the area on April 27, bringing damaging winds, hail, heavy rain, and tornado activity across central Indiana.

In town, residents are also seeing the effects of spring cleanup season. The city’s annual Make Seymour Shine week ran April 13-17, giving households a chance to set out extra trash for curbside pickup as part of the citywide cleanup effort.

Storm Cleanup Continues
The storms late in the week left behind damage across parts of the Seymour area and surrounding Jackson County. National Weather Service reporting confirmed multiple rounds of thunderstorms across central Indiana on April 27, with tornadoes, strong winds, hail, and flooding among the impacts. Local coverage also noted a tornado in Jackson County northwest of Seymour between Bobtown and Cortland, underscoring how widespread the weather damage was.

For many residents, the focus now is on cleanup, repairs, and checking on neighbors. In situations like this, the pace of recovery often depends on how quickly trees, debris, and damaged property can be cleared. The storm also came during a week when the city was already pushing its regular spring cleanup message, which makes the timing especially relevant for local households.

What’s Happening Around Town
Even with storm recovery underway, Seymour’s event calendar is still active. The city calendar lists Crossroads Acoustic Festival for April 24-25 and a City Council meeting on April 27, followed by Crossroads of Cultures on May 2. Those events give the community a few chances to get back into a normal rhythm after a tense weather week.

City Hall has also been sharing broader community planning items, including public comment on the Safer Streets for Seymour transportation safety draft plan. That kind of long-range work matters because Seymour continues to balance growth, traffic, and neighborhood safety while keeping the downtown area active.

Community Feel
Seymour still has the feel of a town that shows up for itself. Between the cleanup effort, downtown events, and public meetings, there is a clear pattern of residents and city leaders trying to keep momentum going even when the weather interrupts the week. The city’s own description emphasizes small-town character, steady growth, and a pro-business approach, and this week’s news fits that balance of resilience and routine.

A simple way to sum up the week: Seymour took a hit from the storms, but the community is already doing what it does best — cleaning up, checking in, and moving forward.

BurgerFest 2026 Details
BurgerFest 2026 marks its 36th year on September 19 in downtown Seymour, featuring the World’s Largest Hamburger Parade, Bun Run, kids’ games, car show, and a burger eating contest. Highlights include live music on the main stage from 2-11 p.m., a Giant Ketchup Slide, carnival rides, and food from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m., with proceeds supporting the Home of the Hamburger Scholarship Program.
